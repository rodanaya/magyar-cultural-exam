## UNIT 2 · Magyarország Történelme
### History of Hungary

```
TIMELINE
│
├── 895–896  HONFOGLALÁS (Conquest)
│   └── 7 tribes led by Árpád occupy the Carpathian Basin
│
├── 1001     ÁLLAMALAPÍTÁS (Foundation of the State)
│   └── I. Szent István — first Christian king, Jan 1, 1001
│       ├── Adopted Roman Catholicism; abandoned paganism
│       ├── Organized county (vármegye) system
│       └── Founded 10 dioceses, bishoprics, archbishoprics
│
├── XI–XIV c. KÖZÉPKOR (Middle Ages)
│   ├── Notable Árpád rulers: Szent László, Könyves Kálmán
│   ├── 1222 — II. András: Aranybulla (rights of nobles)
│   ├── 1241–42 — Tatárjárás (Mongol invasion) · devastated country
│   ├── IV. Béla = "second founder of the homeland" (rebuilt it)
│   └── 1301 — Árpád dynasty extinct; Anjou & Luxembourg kings follow
│
├── XV c.    KÖZÉPKOR — Ottoman battles begin
│   ├── 1456 — Battle of Nándorfehérvár (Belgrade)
│   │   └── Hunyadi János defeats Ottomans → halted Turkish advance 70 years
│   │       (Noon bell commemorates this victory)
│   └── 1458–1490 — Hunyadi Mátyás reign = golden age of Renaissance Hungary
│
├── 1526–1686 TÖRÖK HÓDOLTSÁG (Ottoman Era)
│   ├── Aug 29, 1526 — Battle of Mohács → crushing defeat
│   ├── 1541 — Turks take Buda; country split into 3 parts for ~150 years
│   ├── 1552 — Siege of Eger (notable resistance)
│   └── Sep 2, 1686 — Habsburgs recapture Buda
│
├── 1686–1848 HABSBURG URALOM
│   ├── Reconstruction; country becomes multi-ethnic
│   └── 1703–1711 — Rákóczi Ferenc II leads national uprising
│
├── 1825–1848 REFORMKOR (Reform Era)
│   ├── Birth of modern Hungarian nation; language & culture strengthened
│   ├── Goals: official Hungarian language, dismantle feudalism, civil society
│   └── Key figures:
│       ├── Széchenyi István (1791–1860) "a legnagyobb magyar"
│       │   ├── Founded Hungarian Academy of Sciences (MTA)
│       │   └── Built Chain Bridge (Lánchíd) — first stone bridge Buda–Pest
│       ├── Kossuth Lajos
│       ├── Batthyány Lajos
│       ├── Wesselényi Miklós
│       ├── Deák Ferenc
│       └── Kölcsey Ferenc
│
├── 1848–1849 FORRADALOM ÉS SZABADSÁGHARC
│   ├── Mar 15, 1848 — Revolution erupts in Pest
│   │   ├── March Youth at Pilvax Café; 12 points printed
│   │   ├── Mass rally at National Museum
│   │   ├── Freed Táncsics Mihály from prison
│   │   └── Key figures: Petőfi Sándor, Jókai Mór, Vasvári Pál
│   ├── Apr 11, 1848 — First responsible Hungarian government formed
│   │   └── PM: Batthyány Lajos; Finance: Kossuth Lajos; Justice: Deák Ferenc
│   ├── Habsburgs + Russian Tsar army suppress the revolt
│   ├── Aug 13, 1849 — Görgei Artúr surrenders at Világos
│   └── Oct 6, 1849 — 13 Aradi vértanú executed + Batthyány executed in Pest
│
├── 1867     KIEGYEZÉS (Compromise)
│   ├── Deák Ferenc "a haza bölcse" — key negotiator
│   ├── Austro-Hungarian Monarchy created
│   └── 1867–1914 = another golden age (economic boom)
│
├── 1914–1918 FIRST WORLD WAR
│   ├── Jun 28, 1914 — Archduke Ferenc Ferdinánd assassinated in Sarajevo
│   ├── Jul 28, 1914 — Austria-Hungary declares war on Serbia
│   ├── Opposing: Entente vs Central Powers (Germany, AH, Ottoman, Bulgaria)
│   └── Entente wins
│
├── 1920     TRIANONI BÉKESZERZŐDÉS (Treaty of Trianon)
│   ├── Signed June 4, 1920
│   ├── Hungary lost ~2/3 of territory
│   ├── Population: 18.2M → 7.6M; 3.3M Hungarians left outside borders
│   └── 1920s–30s politicians: Horthy Miklós, Bethlen István, Teleki Pál
│
├── 1939–1945 SECOND WORLD WAR
│   ├── Sep 1, 1939 — Germany attacks Poland; WWII begins
│   ├── Hungary joins Axis Powers in 1941
│   ├── Jan 1943 — 2nd Hungarian Army destroyed at the Don River
│   ├── Mar 19, 1944 — Germany occupies Hungary
│   ├── Holocaust in Hungary: ~500,000–600,000 victims
│   └── Spring 1945 — Germany surrenders; Hungary loses regained territories again
│
├── 1956     FORRADALOM ÉS SZABADSÁGHARC
│   ├── Oct 23, 1956 — peaceful student demonstration in Budapest
│   ├── Armed clashes at Hungarian Radio building
│   ├── Revolutionary government: Nagy Imre
│   ├── Emblematic resistance: Corvin köz, Széna tér
│   ├── Symbol: lyukas zászló (flag with Rákosi coat of arms cut out)
│   ├── Nov 4 — Soviet troops suppress uprising
│   ├── Kádár János appointed leader; Nagy Imre executed
│   └── Communist era: 1956–1989
│
└── 1989–1990 RENDSZERVÁLTÁS (Regime Change)
    ├── Jun 16, 1989 — ceremonial reburial of Nagy Imre
    ├── Peaceful transition; multi-party parliamentary democracy
    ├── Spring 1990 — first free elections; PM: Antall József; President: Göncz Árpád
    └── 1991 — Soviet troops finally leave Hungary
```

## UNIT 4 · Az Alaptörvény Alapvető Intézményei
### Fundamental Institutions of the Constitution

```
ALAPTÖRVÉNY (Fundamental Law / Constitution)
├── Adopted: April 18, 2011 by the National Assembly
├── In force: January 1, 2012
└── Highest legal norm — basis of Hungary's legal system

HÁROM HATALMI ÁG (Three Branches of Power)
│
├── TÖRVÉNYHOZÓ HATALOM — ORSZÁGGYŰLÉS (Legislative — Parliament)
│   ├── Supreme representative body of Hungary
│   ├── 199 members (képviselők)
│   ├── Elected for 4 years
│   └── Key tasks:
│       ├── Creates & amends the Fundamental Law
│       ├── Makes laws
│       ├── Adopts central budget
│       └── Elects the Prime Minister
│
├── VÉGREHAJTÓ HATALOM — KORMÁNY (Executive — Government)
│   ├── Head: Miniszterelnök (Prime Minister) = Orbán Viktor
│   │   ├── Elected by the National Assembly
│   │   └── Represents Hungary in the European Council
│   ├── Members: PM + ministers
│   └── Ministries (14 total): Agriculture, Interior, Energy, Construction & Transport,
│       EU Affairs, Defence, Justice, Public Admin, Culture & Innovation,
│       Foreign Affairs & Trade, PM Cabinet Office, PM's Office, National Economy
│
└── KÖZTÁRSASÁGI ELNÖK (President of the Republic)
    ├── Current: Dr. Sulyok Tamás
    ├── Elected by National Assembly for 5 years (secret ballot)
    ├── Can be re-elected once
    ├── Commander-in-Chief of Hungarian Defence Forces
    ├── Convenes inaugural session of Parliament
    └── Can dissolve Parliament (in cases specified by Fundamental Law)
```

## UNIT 5 · Állampolgári Jogok és Kötelezettségek
### Citizens' Rights and Obligations

```
HUMAN RIGHTS — HISTORICAL MILESTONES
├── Magna Carta Libertatum (England, 1215)
├── Aranybulla (Hungary, 1222)
├── Declaration of Rights of Man & Citizen (France, 1789)
└── European Convention on Human Rights (1950)
    └── Obliges states to ensure listed freedoms for all under their jurisdiction

THREE GENERATIONS OF RIGHTS
├── 1st Generation (civil & political)
│   ├── Right to life
│   ├── Right to personal freedom
│   ├── Freedom of association & assembly
│   ├── Freedom of conscience & religion
│   └── Freedom of speech & press
│
├── 2nd Generation (social & economic)
│   ├── Right to work
│   ├── Right to strike
│   ├── Right to education
│   └── Right to social security
│
└── 3rd Generation (collective & solidarity)
    ├── Right to health & environment
    ├── Children's rights
    ├── Patients' rights
    └── Rights of people with disabilities

FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS in the Alaptörvény
├── Equality before the law
├── Right to life and human dignity
├── Right to a fair trial
├── Freedom of thought, conscience, and religion
├── Freedom of expression
├── Freedom of assembly
├── Right to property and inheritance
└── Right to protection of personal data

CAPITAL PUNISHMENT
└── Abolished in 1990 — Constitutional Court declared it unconstitutional
```
